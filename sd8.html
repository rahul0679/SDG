Introduction

SDG 8 aims to promote sustained, inclusive, and sustainable economic growth, full and productive employment, and decent work for all. It recognizes the essential role of economic growth in driving progress, reducing poverty, and improving living standards, while also emphasizing the importance of creating decent jobs that provide a fair income, safe working conditions, and opportunities for advancement. SDG 8 seeks to address a range of challenges, including unemployment, underemployment, and poor working conditions. It aims to create a more equitable and inclusive labor market, where everyone has the opportunity to find a job that meets their needs and contributes to their well-being. Moreover, SDG 8 recognizes the importance of promoting decent work for all, regardless of gender, age, or other factors. This includes ensuring that workers have their rights protected, including the right to fair wages, safe working conditions, and freedom from discrimination.

Goals

  • The specific targets under SDG 8 include: promoting sustained, inclusive, and sustainable economic growth, including through job creation and productivity growth; achieving full and productive employment and decent work for all, including women, young people, and people with disabilities; improving labor productivity and decent work through skills development and entrepreneurship; reducing the number of people living in poverty and ensuring that everyone has access to basic services like education and healthcare; promoting social justice and inclusion, including by addressing inequalities in income and employment opportunities; protecting workers' rights and promoting safe and healthy working conditions; encouraging entrepreneurship and fostering a dynamic and innovative business environment; and expanding international cooperation and support for developing countries to achieve their SDG 8 goals. These targets aim to address the challenges of unemployment, underemployment, and poor working conditions faced by many people around the world, and to create a more equitable and inclusive labor market where everyone has the opportunity to find a decent job and contribute to economic growth.

Challenges

High unemployment and underemployment rates, poor working conditions, inequality in income and employment opportunities, lack of skills and training, limited access to finance, global economic challenges, social and cultural factors hindering women's participation in the labor market, and weak governance and institutional capacity. These challenges can make it difficult for many people to find decent work and contribute to economic growth. For example, in many developing countries, young people face high rates of unemployment due to a lack of job opportunities and the mismatch between their skills and the demands of the labor market. Additionally, women often face discrimination and barriers to entry in certain industries, limiting their employment opportunities and earning potential.

Solutions

Promoting economic growth, creating decent jobs, addressing inequality, improving education and skills training, expanding access to finance, promoting social justice and inclusion, encouraging international cooperation, supporting sustainable development, and strengthening governance and institutional capacity.
