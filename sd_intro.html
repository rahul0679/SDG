Introduction to SDG

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are 17 global objectives established by the United Nations in 2015 to address pressing issues such as poverty, inequality, climate change, and environmental degradation. Aimed for achievement by 2030, they provide a blueprint for creating a more equitable, sustainable, and prosperous world. Each goal has specific targets and indicators to track progress, promoting collaboration
